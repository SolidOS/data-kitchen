Solid Data Kitchen - macOS first launch
=======================================

This app is not signed with an Apple developer certificate yet, so
macOS blocks the first launch of anything downloaded from the
internet. That block is expected and takes under a minute to clear.
Pick the section for your macOS version:

macOS 15 (Sequoia) or newer
---------------------------
1. Double-click "Solid Data Kitchen.app". macOS refuses to open it -
   that's fine, the attempt registers the app with Security settings.
2. Open System Settings > Privacy & Security and scroll down to the
   Security section.
3. You'll see a note that "Solid Data Kitchen" was blocked. Click
   "Open Anyway" and confirm.
4. Launch the app again. Later launches are normal double-clicks.

macOS 14 (Sonoma) or older
--------------------------
1. Right-click (or Control-click) "Solid Data Kitchen.app" and choose
   Open.
2. In the warning dialog, click Open. (If the dialog has no Open
   button, close it and repeat the right-click > Open once more.)
3. macOS remembers the choice; later launches are normal
   double-clicks.

If macOS says the app is "damaged"
----------------------------------
It isn't - that message is how newer macOS versions describe the
missing signature on a quarantined download. Clear the quarantine
flag in Terminal:

    xattr -cr "/path/to/Solid Data Kitchen.app"

Tip: type  xattr -cr  followed by a space, then drag the app icon
into the Terminal window to fill in the path, and press Return.

Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3/M4) Macs
--------------------------------
This build is Intel (x64). The first launch may offer to install
Rosetta - accept, it's a quick one-time Apple download and the app
runs fine under it.

What the app does on your machine
---------------------------------
Solid Data Kitchen runs a personal data server on your own computer
(localhost only). It makes no outside connections except the ones you
ask for - opening a web resource, connecting to a pod, or checking
for a newer release.

Problems? https://github.com/SolidOS/data-kitchen/issues
